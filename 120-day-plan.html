120-Day Entry Plan

PMI · Senior Manager, Business Process Excellence
Russell Dudek
Candidate operating hypothesis

Entry Principle

Earn the right to standardize. The role should begin by learning where process truth lives, how variation is justified, where ownership breaks, and which measures already have credibility. The first 120 days should produce proof, not a theater of templates.

Work Plan

WindowObjectiveActionsDecision Gate / Output
0–30Decode the operating realityMap sponsors, process owners, markets, functions, Digital/IT/Data, Finance, Regulatory/Compliance, and frontline users. Build a top-level taxonomy; select two cross-functional pilots; baseline outcome, flow, control, economics, adoption, and learning; inventory variants, exceptions, workarounds, and system seams.Business owners accept the pilots; baselines are credible enough to act; open assumptions and data gaps are explicit.
31–60Make variation explicitBuild Process Genome maps: stable core, legitimate variants, suspected mutations, and evidence. Create process contracts covering outcome, customer, owner, decision rights, handoffs, controls, exceptions, systems, data, and measures. Classify variants as mandated, strategic, temporary, or unjustified.Owners agree where variation is necessary and where it is inherited process debt.
61–90Run targeted trialsPrioritize a few interventions by value, risk, friction, feasibility, adoption burden, and reuse. Test explicit hypotheses; simplify before automating; instrument trials; establish frontline feedback and human escalation. Compare with baseline and watch for burden displaced to adjacent functions.Evidence shows what improved, what did not, what shifted elsewhere, and whether behavior changed.
91–120Institutionalize learningEstablish portfolio governance, owner cadence, process-health scorecard, variant arbitration, exception reviews, benefit validation, and sustainment ownership. Define scale and stop criteria. Publish the learning method—not a universal template.Executives align on portfolio logic, ownership, process-health measures, and next-wave priorities.

Guardrails

No improvement theater

Maps and workshops are inputs. Changed decisions, handoffs, controls, flow, economics, and adoption are the outcomes.

No global uniformity fiction

Required market and regulatory variation stays visible and governed—not erased for neatness.

No automation before process clarity

Simplify, remove, and clarify first. Automate only stable work with explicit context and authority.

No benefit without sustainment

A change succeeds only when the business owns it and the behavior survives after project support recedes.
