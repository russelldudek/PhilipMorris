## Compact brand-intelligence record

**Official logo source:** The current PMI corporate site displays the Philip Morris International corporate identity. No logo file is embedded in this candidate campaign because a downloadable official asset with explicit public-campaign usage terms was not verified. The campaign uses a text-only `Russell Dudek × PMI` reference and does not imitate the official lockup.

**Palette:** PMI's public web experience is high-contrast and editorial, relying heavily on white, dark neutral typography, and blue accents. The campaign palette (`#111826`, `#175BD8`, `#08A9C4`, restrained violet/green/amber signals) is an original candidate palette informed by those public cues; it is not represented as PMI's official color specification.

**Typography character:** Large, confident editorial headlines; clean sans-serif body copy; strong hierarchy; substantial whitespace. The campaign uses system sans-serif fonts so no unlicensed font assets are distributed.

**Photography/illustration character:** PMI public pages combine transformation, people, science, products, and progress reporting. Because image licensing and tobacco-product promotion constraints are material, the campaign deliberately avoids republishing product or employee photography.

**Geometry and interface motifs:** Broad editorial panels, measured data points, clear progress indicators, rounded digital controls, and clean modular information architecture.

**Motion character:** PMI's public digital experience is calm, editorial, and evidence-led rather than game-like. The campaign translates that cue into measured scanning, classification, state change, and routed evidence. These are original candidate-created motions—not copied PMI animations.

**Overall vibe:** Global transformation; science and evidence; human judgment; premium corporate editorial; direct discussion of difficult tradeoffs.

**Approved or supportable assets:** Text-only PMI reference, official-source links, original data typography, and original candidate-created diagrams. No copied PMI logo, product image, employee image, or proprietary interface is included.

**Original visuals created:** The Process Genome, its four lenses, the intervention portfolio comparison, the process-health scorecard, and the 120-day sequence.

**Recommended use by artifact:** Strongest employer-adjacent cues on the candidate site and operating artifact; restrained blue/neutral accents in the resume and cover letter; evidence/reporting grammar in briefs and plans.

## Usage boundary

This is an independent candidate campaign. It does not imply PMI endorsement, reproduce proprietary process information, or function as product advertising.
